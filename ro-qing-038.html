Qing Dynasty Kangxi Period 康熙 Blue-and-White Tangmingkuan 堂名款 Scholar's Desk Object Jingdezhen

Fig. 1. The box open: interior of the base covered in a pale bluish-green (fenqing) glaze, alongside the lid, painted in underglaze cobalt with a bearded scholar or Daoist immortal seated among rocks, with books, a staff, and a gourd vessel nearby.

Object Description

A small round box with a flat lid, intended for the storage of red seal paste. The interior surface is covered in a pale glaze with a light bluish-green (fenqing 粉青) tint. Objects of this kind belonged to the culture of the scholar's studio and were used together with brushes, ink, seals, and paper.

The lid is painted with an elderly, bearded scholar or Daoist immortal in loose robes. The figure is seated among rocks; nearby are books, a staff, and a gourd vessel. The painting is executed in a manner characteristic of the Kangxi period: free cobalt washes are combined with fine linear rendering of the figure and details.

Mark & Attribution

On the underside of the lid is a four-character studio mark, 安素草堂 (Ānsù Cǎotáng, "Studio/Pavilion of Simple Elegance"). This is not an imperial reign mark but a tangmingkuan 堂名款 — a mark of a private studio or pavilion. Such marks were used by educated patrons, officials, and collectors who commissioned high-quality porcelain in Jingdezhen. The absence of the character 製 (zhì, "made") suggests the inscription may not be a production mark but rather an ownership or commemorative one — consistent with the tradition of scholar's-studio objects of the late Kangxi period.

Diagnostic Features

The underglaze cobalt sits deep beneath the transparent glaze and does not project above the surface. Gradations of tone are observed within the blue passages, ranging from saturated sapphire to pale bluish-grey — an effect consistent with brushwork on the raw (unfired) body, subsequently covered with glaze and fired at high temperature. The glaze is transparent, deep, and naturally lustrous. At the base and the seating edge, unglazed porcelain body is visible: pale, greyish-beige, and fine-grained. The edges carry a soft patina consistent with prolonged domestic and collection handling.

  • Underglaze cobalt set deep beneath a transparent glaze, with tonal gradation.
  • Free cobalt washes combined with fine linear figure detail, characteristic of Kangxi painting.
  • Studio (tangmingkuan) mark rather than an imperial reign mark, without the character 製.
  • Pale, greyish-beige, fine-grained unglazed body at the base.
  • Soft, naturally accumulated edge patina.
  • Pale bluish-green (fenqing) interior glaze.
No dedicated photomicrographs were available for this object at the time of documentation; the diagnostic features above are recorded from direct optical examination at 10×–40× and are presented here for completeness of the record.

Conclusion

The blue-and-white box KX-DSB-B&W-01 belongs to the circle of late-Kangxi scholar's-studio objects. The studio mark 安素草堂, the quality of the cobalt painting, the pale porcelain body, the transparent glaze, and the natural patina support the attribution: China, Jingdezhen, Kangxi period, c. 1700–1722.

Summary attribution: Porcelain seal-paste box with blue-and-white painting and the studio mark 安素草堂. Jingdezhen, late Kangxi period. Authenticity is supported by the convergence of stylistic, epigraphic, and optical-microscopic features.
