Qing Dynasty Kangxi Period 康熙 Doucai 斗彩 Peaches & Bats Motif Scholar's Desk Object Jingdezhen

Fig. 1. Lid, painted in doucai technique: branches bearing three peaches, green leaves, a flower, and a stylized bat at the center. Underglaze cobalt forms the principal blue masses and outlines; overglaze enamels supply the colored detail.

Object Description

A round box with a slightly domed lid. The decoration is executed in doucai technique: branches with peaches, green leaves, a flower, and stylized bats. The principal blue masses and contours are executed in underglaze cobalt; the colored details are supplied by overglaze enamels.

The subject carries a pronounced auspicious programme. The peach, shoutao 寿桃, symbolizes longevity, while the bat, bianfu 蝙蝠, is a visual rebus for the word fu 福 — happiness, fortune, and wellbeing. This combination is especially characteristic of objects associated with scholarly and court culture of the Kangxi period.

Technique of Execution

The decoration is built on the combination of underglaze blue and overglaze enamels. The cobalt areas form the compositional framework: the larger leaves, contours, and background masses. Iron-red, green, and pinkish enamels are applied over the transparent glaze.

Under the microscope, the enamel reads as a distinct layer, sitting slightly proud of the glaze surface. This confirms the two-stage firing required by the doucai technique: a first high-temperature firing with cobalt and glaze, and a second, low-temperature firing with the enamels.

Mark & Epigraphy

The base bears the six-character mark 大清康熙年製 within a double circle. In format it is close to the mark on box KX-DSB-DC-02, but the characters are more closely spaced — possibly indicating the hand of a different calligrapher-painter within the same workshop tradition. The strokes of the mark show natural variation in thickness and pressure. The underglaze position of the cobalt is confirmed by the fact that the pigment is covered by the transparent glaze and does not sit on the surface.

Diagnostic Features

The glaze is transparent, deep, with the characteristic clarity of Kangxi-period surfaces. The interior is covered in a pale fenqing glaze. The cobalt is saturated, in places dense, with a soft internal granularity. The colored enamels occupy the correct surface position and do not appear to be later overpainting.

The porcelain body at the footring is pale, dense, and fine-grained. The edges show soft, natural ageing. A network of fine craquelure is moderately expressed and lacks the regularity characteristic of artificial ageing.

  • Transparent, deep glaze with characteristic Kangxi surface clarity; pale fenqing interior.
  • Saturated underglaze cobalt with soft internal granularity, forming the compositional framework.
  • Overglaze enamels sitting proud of the glaze, in correct surface position — the doucai two-firing marker.
  • Pale, dense, fine-grained porcelain body at the footring.
  • Six-character Kangxi mark within a double circle, with natural hand-brushed stroke variation.
  • Moderate, irregular fine craquelure network, without artificial regularity.
No dedicated photomicrographs were available for this object at the time of documentation; the diagnostic features above are recorded from direct optical examination at 10×–40× and are presented here for completeness of the record.

Conclusion

Box KX-DSB-DC-03 is an example of Kangxi-period doucai cabinet ware bearing the auspicious motif of longevity and happiness. The technique of execution, the Kangxi mark, the glaze structure, and the character of the cobalt and enamels are consistent with Jingdezhen production of the late Kangxi period.

Summary attribution: Porcelain seal-paste box decorated with peaches of longevity and bats, doucai technique. Jingdezhen, Kangxi period, c. 1700–1722. Authenticity is supported by the convergence of optical-microscopic, technological, epigraphic, and iconographic features.
