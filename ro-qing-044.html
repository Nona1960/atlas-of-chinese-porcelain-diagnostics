RO-QING-044

Peacock Blue-Green Vase with a Bogu Medallion

"In Memory of Kangxi" Series / Ji Nian Kangxi 紀念康熙 — Vase 4 of 4

Qing Dynasty · Yongzheng Period, 1723–1735 Jingdezhen bangchui ping 棒槌瓶 Co²⁺ / Cu²⁺ hybrid system Mark: 大清雍正年製

Description

The fourth and concluding vase of the "In Memory of Kangxi" series presents the most chemically complex chromophore system of the ensemble. The rich peacock blue-green ground (kongque lü 孔雀綠) combines an underglaze or partially sub-glaze cobalt layer with a copper-green modulation, indicating at least two technologically distinct firing stages.

The vessel's form belongs to the rouleau, or bangchui ping type — a cylindrical shape closely associated with Kangxi-period blue-and-white porcelain. During the Yongzheng period this form was reinterpreted through a coloured ground and a medallion composition.

A large white medallion in the bogu 博古 antiquarian style occupies the body: it depicts an archaic vase with gilt ring handles in mask form, a floral border at the neck, and a mythical beast (qilin) amid waves and clouds at the base. At the shoulder, a smaller round medallion carries a floral motif (peony or lotus) on the lid of a miniature vessel. The execution is finer and more restrained than the Kangxi prototype, in keeping with Yongzheng court taste.

Chemical and Technological Interpretation

The peacock ground represents a hybrid chromophore system. The primary blue tonality is linked to cobalt Co²⁺, applied before or during the first high-temperature firing. The green modulation is linked to copper Cu²⁺, applied as an overglaze or partially overglaze layer during a subsequent firing.

This combination required a complex technological sequence: first, the formation of the porcelain body, the clear glaze and the cobalt layer; then, the application of the copper-green modulation and the medallion's decorative details. This makes the peacock vase the most technically complex object within the four-piece series.

Base Mark

The base bears an underglaze cobalt-blue mark in a double oval cartouche, four characters in two columns: 大清雍正年製 ("Made during the Yongzheng reign of the Great Qing"). The unglazed foot rim shows the characteristic darkening and mottling at the glaze junction consistent with the period.

Microscopy

Gemmological optical microscopy of the peacock ground surface (area away from the mark) records the overall glaze texture: an uneven distribution of minute bubbles of varying size, localized reflections, and a slight granularity of tone within the blue-green range. The surface shows the irregularity typical of a lead-alkaline glaze — smooth gradations of colour density without sharp mechanical boundaries.

The image was captured under an optical microscope and shows the general surface texture of the glaze. It does not capture the medallion border, so the specific quantitative characteristics of a diffusion halo and individual pigment aggregate size are not confirmed by this frame and are not stated here; verification of these features would require a separate image of the border zone at higher magnification.

Interpretation

Diagnostic Features

  • underglaze cobalt mark 大清雍正年製 in a double oval cartouche, blue tone, without sharp mechanical brush-stroke boundaries
  • hybrid chromophore system of the ground: cobalt Co²⁺ (blue base) + copper Cu²⁺ (green modulation)
  • overall glaze texture under microscopy — uneven bubble distribution, smooth colour gradations
  • evidence of a multi-stage firing sequence: high-temperature firing for the body and underglaze cobalt, followed by a separate firing for the copper modulation
  • antiquarian (bogu) decorative programme in the medallion with a mythical beast — stylistically consistent with Yongzheng court taste
  • darkening and mottling of the unglazed foot rim, consistent with the age and handling history of the piece

Conclusion

The peacock blue-green ground vase concludes the "In Memory of Kangxi" series and presents the most technically complex chromophore system in the ensemble. The hybrid combination of underglaze cobalt and overglaze copper modulation, the antiquarian medallion programme, and the character of the underglaze mark are consistent with a Yongzheng-period attribution.

Full confirmation of the attribution requires further analysis: XRF elemental analysis to precisely determine the composition of the glaze and pigments, gemmological microscopy of the medallion border zone to verify cobalt diffusion, and thermoluminescence (TL) dating of the body for independent confirmation of firing age.

China, Jingdezhen, Yongzheng period, 1723–1735. A porcelain vase of bangchui ping form from the four-piece "In Memory of Kangxi" series, with a hybrid Co²⁺/Cu²⁺ peacock blue-green ground and a white bogu medallion depicting an archaic vase, floral ornament, and a mythical beast amid waves. The attribution is supported by the character of the underglaze mark, the hybrid chromophore system, the glaze microtexture, and the decorative programme; XRF analysis, microscopy of the border zone, and TL dating are recommended for final confirmation.
