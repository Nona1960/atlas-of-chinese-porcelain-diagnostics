Reference Object ro-song-005

Guan-type Brush Washer with Lotus and Stamen Relief

官窑型 — Southern Song Dynasty, 12th–13th century

Southern Song Guan Ware Lotus Relief Multi-layer Glaze Orange-Peel Texture Golden Wire Effect Firing Halo GOM Examined

Object Description

A brush washer of shallow circular form with a slightly everted rim, compressed globular body, and a low narrow foot ring. The interior preserves a sharply moulded lotus composition in high relief. The glaze is of turquoise-celadon tone, applied in multiple thick layers producing considerable optical depth and a characteristic jade-like translucency. A continuous gold-ochre band surrounds the rim, consistent with the classical Iron-Foot / Golden Wire effect of Southern Song Guan ware.

The foot ring is narrow, cream-white in body, and unglazed on its contact surface. The glazed base interior carries a fine crackle pattern continuous with the exterior glaze system. The overall condition is exceptional, with all decorative and technological features preserved in outstanding clarity.

Microscopic Diagnostic Analysis

The brush washer was examined using Gemmological Optical Microscopy (GOM) under reflected, oblique and UV illumination at magnifications from 20× to 100×. The investigation focused on the interaction between the glaze and the moulded lotus relief, the firing behaviour of the multi-layer Guan glaze, foot-rim morphology, firing support traces, and microscopic oxidation features. Observations were compared with published instrumental studies of authenticated Southern Song official Guan ware.

The object demonstrates an exceptionally coherent system of technological markers. The simultaneous preservation of decorative features (lotus relief, glaze–relief interaction) and manufacturing evidence (orange-peel texture, firing halo, iron-oxide speckling) within a single piece provides an unusually complete diagnostic record.

1. Lotus Relief Morphology

The interior preserves a sharply moulded lotus composition comprising a central seed-pod (lianpeng), pearl-bead ring, and two concentric rows of rectangular lotus petals. Microscopy demonstrates precise relief geometry with no evidence of mould misalignment or later recutting. The quality of moulding is consistent with imperial kiln production standards.

2. Glaze Behaviour over High Relief

One of the most diagnostic observations is the behaviour of the multi-layer glaze across the relief summits. Microscopy reveals progressive glaze thinning at relief peaks with partial exposure of the porcelain body, clean glaze-to-body transitions, and complete absence of chipping or abrasion. The exposed porcelain body is fine-grained, dense, and iron-poor. This pattern could only develop during original high-temperature firing and represents an important authentication criterion.

3. Multi-Layer Glaze Microstructure

Under oblique illumination the glaze exhibits the characteristic microstructure of repeated-application Song Guan glaze: considerable thickness, smooth optical depth, gradual pooling, and homogeneous vitreous texture. The glaze behaves as a continuous optical medium rather than a single thin coating, consistent with multiple sequential glaze applications during manufacture.

4. Orange-Peel Microtexture

Microscopy reveals a characteristic orange-peel microtexture with gentle undulations of approximately 50–200 μm wavelength uniformly distributed across the glaze surface. No polishing marks are present. This texture records differential surface tension during cooling of multiple glaze layers and is regarded as one of the characteristic microscopic signatures of authentic Southern Song Guan ware. It cannot be reproduced by single-layer application or modern firing schedules.

5. Gold-Ochre Foot-Rim Band — 铁线口

A continuous amber to gold-ochre band surrounds the glaze termination zone at the rim. Microscopically this band demonstrates gradual colour transition, body-integrated pigmentation, glaze-flow accumulation, and iron enrichment at the glaze-body interface. The feature represents the classical Golden Wire / Iron Foot effect of Song Guan ware. Artificial colour application is excluded by the body-integrated character of the pigmentation.

6. Iron-Oxide Speckling and Firing Halo

The unglazed foot contains numerous embedded iron-oxide crystallites with irregular distribution, orange-brown coloration, and complete integration within the clay matrix rather than superficial staining. The oxidation developed during firing. A circular firing halo with central concentration of iron oxidation and gradual decrease toward the margins is present on the base interior, corresponding to contact with a sand-pad firing support. This feature is considered one of the most reliable microscopic indicators of authentic Southern Song Guan production.

7. Optical Glaze Depth and UV Fluorescence

The glaze demonstrates pronounced optical depth with jade-like translucency, internal light scattering, and gradual colour modulation optically enhanced in thicker glaze zones. Long-wave UV examination reveals uniform pale blue-violet fluorescence with no discontinuities, no evidence of restoration, and no reglazed areas, confirming the homogeneity of the original glaze throughout.

Diagnostic Interpretation

Authentication of the brush washer is based on the agreement of numerous independent microscopic observations reconstructing the complete technological history from moulding and glazing through kiln firing to long-term preservation. No single feature alone is decisive; the high internal consistency of all observations provides strong evidence for authentic Southern Song official Guan ware.
